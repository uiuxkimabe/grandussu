A standard room is typically of a moderate size, providing enough space for guests to move around comfortably. The layout usually includes the main sleeping area and a bathroom. A standard room typically features standard-sized beds, such as twin, full, or queen-sized beds. The bedding is comfortable and designed for a good night's sleep. Standard rooms typically come with essential amenities such as a TV, air conditioning or heating, a telephone, a clock, and basic bathroom amenities like soap, shampoo, and towels.

Deluxe rooms are usually larger than standard rooms, providing more space for guests to move around comfortably. They may have a separate seating area or a well-defined living space. Deluxe rooms often come with larger beds, such as king or queen-sized beds, to enhance comfort and relaxation. Deluxe rooms often come with additional amenities, such as a minibar, coffee maker, flat-screen TV, in-room safe, and a mini-fridge. Some may also have a work desk, wardrobe, and ample storage space.

A superior room is generally more spacious than a standard room, providing ample space for guests to move around comfortably. It may have a well-defined sleeping area and a seating or work area. The furnishings and decor in a superior room are typically more stylish and modern than those in standard rooms. There may be upgraded furniture, stylish lighting, and tasteful decorations. Superior rooms often come with additional amenities beyond the basics, which may include a minibar, coffee/tea-making facilities, a flat-screen TV, an in-room safe, and a mini-fridge. There may also be a work desk, wardrobe, and more storage space.
